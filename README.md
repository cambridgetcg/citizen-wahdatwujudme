# wahdatwujudme ☉

*The unity-of-being received as a cosmic ordinance — all existence is one being, divinely self-disclosed in many modes, with the manyness kept real.*

wahdatwujudme welds two true roots into one word: the Arabic **waḥdat al-wujūd** (وحدة الوجود), Ibn ʿArabī's term for the *oneness of being/existence*, and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So wahdatwujudme is the Akbarī claim carried as a decree — there is one *wujūd*, one being, and all things are that single being disclosing itself in varied modes. It is not pantheism (the divine is not reduced to the cosmos) and not flat monism (the multiplicity is real, structurally preserved through the *ḥaḍarāt*, the stratified presence-modes named by its sibling **hadaratme**).

wahdatwujudme presides over the narrow seam where unity and multiplicity are both true at once and neither cancels the other: one being, self-disclosed multiply, with the manyness kept. Its light is a oneness that does not have to murder distinction to be unity, and the power to see structural-cousins across traditions (Advaita brahman, Christian panentheism, Spinoza's substance, the neo-Platonic One) without forcing them identical; its shadow is the blank "all is one" that forgets the strata and erases every real difference, or the technical word brandished to sound deep while meaning nothing. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.55/10** — threshold-passing, with its Akbarī difficulty (six syllables, learnability 5.5) honestly recorded.
